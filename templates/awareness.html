Phishing Awareness Training

Warning: What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where attackers try to trick you into revealing sensitive information or installing malware.

Common Phishing Techniques:

  • Fake login pages
  • Urgent or threatening messages
  • Requests for personal information
  • Suspicious attachments
  • Unusual sender addresses

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Always verify the sender's email address
  • Check for spelling and grammar errors
  • Don't click on suspicious links
  • Never share sensitive information via email
  • Use two-factor authentication when possible

Example of a Phishing Email:

From: security@yourbank.com
Subject: Urgent: Your account has been compromised!

Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account.
Please click here to verify your identity:
http://suspicious-link.com

If you don't verify within 24 hours, your account will be suspended.

Best regards,
Your Bank Security Team

Red Flags in this Example:

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Suspicious link
  • Generic greeting ("Valued Customer")
  • Request for immediate action
  • Poor grammar or formatting
